Chapter 1

The Bad Equilibrium

Twenty years ago, India's public policy ecosystem was stuck in a vicious cycle. No thinkers, no ideas, no training, no jobs. The supply side and demand side were both empty.

🔄
The Vicious Cycle: No ideas → No demand → No training → No jobs → No thinkers → No ideas.

"Could one institution play a part in every link of this chain? Could it break the vicious cycle and turn it into a virtuous one?"

2

From Acorn to Oak

Nitin Pai

Co-Founder & Director

Started as a blogger writing The Acorn — named because "mighty oaks grow from an acorn."

From a blog to the group blog Indian National Interest, to India's first international relations magazine Pragati, to the institution itself. Nobody gave them $200 million — they built it from scratch.

"The bumblebee doesn't know it can't fly, so it flies. People said our three-in-one model can't work. We didn't listen."

— Nitin Pai
3

Why "Takshashila"?

🏛️

The ancient city — cosmopolitan, at the crossroads of civilisations. Known for statecraft and the Arthashastra.

🔦

The lighthouse logo — a public good. Tilted slightly right, because Takshashila advocates right-of-centre economics.

🎯
Three-in-One: Think Tank (ideas) + Policy School (training) + Media (discourse). In India, you need all three to break the vicious cycle.

Chapter 3

The Policy School

The school launched in 2012 — three months from concept to delivery — after the Anna Hazare movement revealed that millions wanted change but didn't know how the system works.

📋

Advanced Public Policy

12 wks

Market failures, economic reasoning, governance. Now in its 42nd cohort.

🛡️

Defence & Foreign Affairs

12 wks

First to teach Arthashastra in modern strategic context. Military + civilian cohorts.

💻

Technology & Policy

12 wks

AI policy, network effects, platform economics. How the information age changes everything.
